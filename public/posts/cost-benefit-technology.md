Every platform decision I have made in the last two decades — ERP, LMS, BI, infrastructure — started with the same question: *what does each alternative actually cost, and what does it actually deliver?*

## Neither evangelism nor loyalty

I run a deliberately hybrid stack. Our academic and administrative ERPs run on .NET and MSSQL. Our LMS is Moodle. Our service desk is ERPNext. Our dashboards are Metabase next to Qlikview. None of that is ideology — all of it is arithmetic.

The framework is simple to state and hard to practice:

1. **Total cost, honestly counted** — licensing, but also migration, training, integration, and the cost of the skills you will need on payroll.
2. **Functionality against *your* requirements** — not the feature matrix, your actual workflows.
3. **Exit cost** — what it takes to leave. Lock-in is a price; put a number on it.
4. **The capability dividend** — what your team learns by operating it.

## Where open source wins — and where it does not

Open source wins when the capability dividend is high and you have (or can build) the team to collect it. Proprietary wins when the problem is solved, the vendor is accountable, and your team's attention is better spent elsewhere.

The discipline is refusing to know the answer before doing the analysis. That refusal, sustained over twenty years, is the closest thing I have to a methodology.
